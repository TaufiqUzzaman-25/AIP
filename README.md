                                                                  AI Assistant for Prescription (AIP)

The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in healthcare has the potential to significantly improve clinical efficiency and reduce human error. This thesis presents an AI-driven system, “AI Assistant for Prescription (AIP)”, designed to support automated prescription digitization and medication management.The proposed framework leverages the Gemini 2.5 model to process prescription images and extract structured medical information, including medication names, dosage schedules, and instructions.The system adopts a modular architecture consisting of image preprocessing, model inference, and output parsing components to ensure scalability and robustness. Advanced natural language processing techniques and prompt engineering are utilized to improve the accuracy and consistency of extracted prescription data. To address challenges related to model reliability and integration,optimization strategies are applied to handle API latency and ensure stable system performance. Experimental observations indicate that the proposed system improves medication adherence and reduces manual interpretation errors compared to traditional approaches.The results demonstrate the feasibility of deploying AI-based prescription assistance systems in real-world mobile healthcare environments. The proposed approach provides a foundation for future research in intelligent healthcare automation and patient-centric digital health solutions. Index Terms— Artificial Intelligence, Prescription Digitization, Mobile Health (mHealth), Natural Language Processing, Healthcare Informatics, Android Application.
